# Minnesota Tennis Players Council (MTPC / "Kenny Tenny") - Program Objectives

## Organization Overview

The Minnesota Tennis Players Council (MTPC)—affectionately known as "Kenny Tenny"—is a community-driven adult tennis collective serving the Twin Cities. What began as a small group of passionate players has grown into a vibrant community of approximately 100 participants, ages 25–65, who come together year-round to play competitive tennis, build relationships, and uplift the broader tennis community.

## Mission Statement

**"Tennis is what we are, and fun is how we do it."**

We foster a healthy, supportive, and welcoming environment where players of advanced skill levels can play frequently, socialize, mentor others, and contribute to the growth of tennis in Minnesota.

### Programs and Activities

**Year-Round Tennis**:
MTPC offers high-level tennis 4–6 days per week across all seasons, as well as league play, community events, and charitable activities.

**Summer Programming**:
- **Tuesday/Thursday Doubles Sessions**: 4–5 courts of 4.5–5.0 level players at a local high school
- Balls and drinks provided
- Weekly Tuesday grill outs hosted by members
- **USTA Team Leagues**:
  - Wednesday: 18+ 4.5 Team
  - Thursday: 18+ 5.0 Team
  - Thursday: 55+ 4.5 Team
  - Play hosted at premium clubs

**Winter Programming**:
- **Indoor Tennis**: Tuesday/Thursday tennis at local indoor clubs
- **Winter League Play**:
  - Wednesday: 18+ 4.5 league
  - Thursday: 18+ 5.0 league
- **Friday Performance Sessions**: Adult play incorporating performance juniors to foster mentorship, support, and competitive development

**Community Activities**:
- Actively support The Annual Holiday Mixer raising funds for Inner City Tennis
- Group trips to regional and national tournaments
- Occasional community socials and tennis events

### Community Impact

**1. Access to Advanced Tennis**
Players have competitive opportunities most days of the week, promoting healthy lifestyles and lifelong physical activity.

**2. Mentorship and Networking**
MTPC organically fosters mentorship relationships:
- Between established business professionals and younger adults (ages 22–35)
- Between advanced adult players and high-performing junior athletes
- This strengthens both personal and professional growth within the community

**3. Financial Support for Players**
We ensure that cost is not a barrier to participation by providing:
- League fee subsidies
- Indoor court time support
- Tennis balls and equipment
- Racquet stringing service (reduced cost and 24-hour turnaround)

**4. Community Engagement & Charity**
Our members contribute time, resources, and gear to junior and adult players throughout the year:
- **Annual Holiday Mixer**: Supports Inner City Tennis
- **InnerCity Tennis Foundation**: Serves 6,000 kids annually across 22 public parks in the summer, 10+ after school programs (fall to spring), and at ICT's indoor tennis center in Minneapolis since 1973
- **St. Paul Urban Tennis Golf Event**: Annual support of tennis development
- **Saint Paul Urban Tennis (SPUT)**: Dynamic nonprofit dedicated to transforming the lives of youth in underserved communities of St. Paul since 1991
- **Reciprocal Inclusion**: Thunder Bay Ontario players included in events (including Mid Canada Tennis Classic)
